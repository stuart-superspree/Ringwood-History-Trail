800th Anniversary

Royal Market Charter · 1226–2026

⚜️

Granted by King Henry III · 1226

In March 1226 King Henry III granted Ringwood the right to hold a market on Wednesdays. That market has traded every single Wednesday since — one of England's longest unbroken market traditions, now reaching its 800th year.

⏳ A Charter Through the Ages
1226
King Henry III grants Wednesday market rights. Two annual fairs also established — on St Peter's Day (29 June) and St Andrew's Day (30 November).
1553
Edward VI formally confirms the market charter. Ringwood becomes the main produce centre across the Avon and Stour valleys, famed for New Forest ponies.
1752
Calendar reform changes fair days to 10 July and 11 December. The fairs continue as Ringwood highlights until the Second World War.
1868
The Town Hall is built overlooking the Market Place by Mr Morant, Lord of the Manor — cementing the market as the civic heart of Ringwood.
1988
The cattle market at the Furlong closes after centuries of livestock trading alongside the Wednesday market.
2026
800th anniversary of the Royal Charter. Ringwood celebrates eight centuries of unbroken market tradition with a major community celebration on Sunday 12 July.
